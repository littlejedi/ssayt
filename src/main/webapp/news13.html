Science Education and motivating young students to pursue a scientific career

Prof. Dr. Eva-Maria Neher

XLAB Goettingen Experimental Laboratory fort the Youth, Goettingen, Germany

XLAB provides science education, which bridges the gap between high school and university. One of our principle aims is to raise student’s interest in science and encourage students to pursue scientific careers.

At XLAB, school classes or individual students can perform hands-on experiments in Biology, Ecology, Neuroscience, Chemistry, and Physics with state-of-the-art-equipment under professional guidance. XLAB`s general aims are promoting the attractiveness of science education, promoting the mobility of the students, and encouraging them to take up university studies abroad.

Our missions are:

Professionalism
The XLAB tries to provide an atmosphere of genuine research laboratories with authentic tools and equipment and, most importantly, with lecturers, who are experienced scientists.

Intensity
Students concentrate on one subject, without interruptions by other activities as during an ordinary day at school. This is prerequisite for intensive learning comparable with university teaching.

Internationality
Science is international, and the scientific community resembles a worldwide family. The XLAB International Science Camp conveys this experience to our future scientists.

The target groups are:

1. School classes from Germany, neighboring countries and worldwide. Groups stay for one to 10 days or even longer according to special arrangements.

2. High school and 1st year university students coming individually, attending weekly courses during school holidays in winter, spring and autumn.

3. International students participating in the XLAB Science Camp for three weeks during the summer holidays. The number of students representing one nationality is limited to 4 to 5 in order to avoid the formation of subgroups.

International Science Camps

Since 2003 XLAB organizes International Science Camps for senior High School students and first year University students. Meanwhile we organize two camps every year in summer.

For a period of three weeks, every year 50-60 students carry out various experiments in Biology, Ecology, Chemistry, Physics and Neurosciences

Each student chooses three courses, which last one week. Experienced scientists and professional technical assistants supervise the experimental courses. The general idea behind all courses is to give the students the chance to carry out experiments with sophisticated research equipment.

Young people from Germany, Europe and from all over the world participate in the international science camp in Goettingen. Besides the scientific education, they experience also a cultural exchange, which interconnects the students beyond the duration of the camp. The young students get an early impression what it means to become a member of a scientific community
